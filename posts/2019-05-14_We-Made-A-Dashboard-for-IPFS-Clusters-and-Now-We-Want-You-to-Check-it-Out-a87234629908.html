Now, coming to the premise of the post, one of the main issues that people using IPFS face today is how to manage multiple numbers of IPFS nodes?

To address this problem, the creators of IPFS came up with IPFS Cluster.

IPFS Cluster adds a layer on top of IPFS client which helps us to orchestrate the pinned objects on the IPFS nodes.

Using IPFS Cluster you can do a ton of things:

  • Add/Remove peers(nodes) from a cluster.
  • Add/Remove pins(data) to the nodes. The pins get evenly distributed according to the space left on that a peer, replication factor, etc.
  • Monitor the help of the peers(nodes).
